Regenerative asset shelf

C-Hours make care visible.

A C-Hour is the civic receipt layer the incomplete ledger is missing: one verified hour of useful contribution, made visible without turning care into a casino or private life into a scoreboard.

Read the shapeRead the legal boundary
C-Hour status: concept only. The ambition is real; issuance is not. No receipt, token, credit, donation, membership or financial product is being issued here.

Plain version

One hour should not vanish.

The source memo argues that modern ledgers miss the work that keeps society alive: care, repair, mentoring, volunteering, disaster preparation and ecological stewardship. This page gives that missing layer a public shape.

Receipt

Record the hour

A C-Hour would record verified public-good contribution. It would need source evidence, consent, human review and a correction path.

Not money

No passive yield

The concept does not belong on the site as a speculative coin, yield product, bank substitute or trading instrument.

Privacy

Consent first

Private care and household work must not be turned into a public scoreboard by default. Public recognition needs opt-in rules.

Governance

Law before launch

Any real pilot would need legal review, anti-fraud design, local authority, privacy controls and public governance before issuance.

Translation from the DOCX

De-escalated for public use.

The DOCX uses the term "Regenerative Asset" and argues for a legislative carve-out in a US digital-asset context. This website does not present that as current law. It keeps the useful public idea: verified contribution needs a better receipt layer, and that layer must avoid speculation, laundering, fake value, exploitation and privacy harm.

  • Care economy: recognise useful work that normal money-ledgers often ignore.
  • Braided economy: keep ordinary money separate from contribution records instead of flattening everything into one score.
  • Public ledger: make records inspectable where public visibility is chosen and lawful.
  • Human review: keep agents as helpers, not unchallengeable authorities.
Oceania question

Could contribution be regional without becoming extractive?

Oceania has shared pressures: climate adaptation, care, disaster response, food waste, ocean stewardship and local resilience. A C-Hour-style receipt could become a research question for those pressures, but only if each place controls its own data, law, recognition rules and cultural boundaries.
