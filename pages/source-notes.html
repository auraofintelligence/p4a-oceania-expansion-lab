Source handling

What was used, and what was softened.

The site is built from the existing P4A website style and a C-Hour / national-security DOCX. The public copy has been translated into Australian English and intentionally de-escalated.

Source status: the DOCX is used as a concept engine, not current authority. The point is to translate its force without laundering uncertainty.
Primary style source

P4A website scaffold

The visual language comes from `p4a_xyz`: dark royal purple, amethyst, magenta, gold and civic workbench cards. This lab shifts the accent toward deep-water blue and reef cyan for the Oceania layer.

The P4A source also supplied the strongest boundary line: P4A is a proposed movement and drafting project, not a registered political party.

Concept source

C-Hour legislation DOCX

The DOCX explores C-Hours, regenerative assets, digital asset law, decentralised compute, Oceania infrastructure, health and space-scale scenarios. It is written in a strong US-English strategic memo voice.

This site does not reproduce that voice exactly. It extracts a public website shape with the force intact: C-Hours as contribution receipts, Oceania as a live design field, decentralised compute as civic resilience and the moonshot material as a labelled surge shelf.

Review-needed claims

What needs checking later.

  • Current Australian, New Zealand and Pacific electoral law.
  • Any claim about US digital-asset legislation or regulatory categories.
  • Any C-Hour legal definition, issuance model, ledger design or public-good receipt pathway.
  • Food waste, dementia, health technology, infrastructure and procurement figures.
  • Medical claims about sauna, HBOT, detoxification, cognition or biological optimisation.
  • Any space, ISS, orbital, disaster or geophysical proposal.
Public copy rule

Say less, mark more.

The safest public posture is: label what is source-backed, label what is speculative, label what needs review, and do not pretend an idea has authority just because it has a good website.
