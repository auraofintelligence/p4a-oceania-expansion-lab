Participant roles

Enter where your energy is real.

The system does not need everyone to become the same kind of political person. It needs many kinds of sovereign participation that can see each other without flattening each other.

Care

Care witnesses

Make care visible without cheapening it: family support, elder care, disability support, child care, peer support, mental health first response and neighbour checks.

Code

Stack builders

Build local pages, kiosks, dashboards, mesh tools, source trails, open datasets, Legal RAG notes, digital twins and repairable civic interfaces.

Culture

Signal artists

Turn policy fog into songs, games, memes, comics, films, rituals, explainers, public challenges and joyful civic culture that people actually want to enter.

Land

Place stewards

Map water, food, fire, flood, transport, waste, habitat, repair work, local materials and the living systems that electoral borders barely understand.

Proof

Receipt keepers

Future

Space commons crew

Train the imagination upward: orbital commons, solar weather, autonomous robotics, planetary defence, open science and peaceful space treaty thinking.

The stance

Sovereign people, shared quests.

Self-sovereign people need a clean interface where they can see the quests, inspect the receipts, bring their own tools and leave with their dignity intact.

The invitation is competence: learn the stack, fork the parts that work, reject the parts that smell wrong, and help build a civic commons strong enough for the century you actually live in.

First build moves

Pick a small proof and make it real.

  • Map one local pain point: food waste, unsafe transport, dead public space, care overload, flood risk, youth boredom, digital exclusion or broken trust.
  • Create one public receipt: what was done, who checked it, what changed, what remains unknown and how someone corrects it.
  • Build one visible door: a page, poster, QR trail, event, game, song, kiosk, dashboard or shared table that someone else can enter.
  • Keep one private vault: your diary, health, family, identity and home context stay yours unless you deliberately choose otherwise.
