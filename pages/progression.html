Progression with lift

Seed, fork, surge, commons.

The pathway is a living sequence: seed the pattern in Australia, fork it locally, translate it across Oceania, train the civic reserve, then aim the surplus toward peaceful planetary work.

Read the stages Open trust stack
Progression is a route map for sovereign nodes. Every place keeps its own names, law, protocol, source memory and right of refusal.

Staged pathway

Each layer adds voltage.

The route starts with proof, not permission. A local node earns trust through receipts. A regional layer earns trust by making those local nodes stronger without swallowing them.

Stage 0

Australia seed

P4A is the named spark: roots-up democracy, public ledgers, lawful review, culture, care, transparent tools and the refusal to leave civic imagination to tired institutions.

Stage 1

Self-similar local nodes

A street, island, club, school, co-op, youth crew or council area can run a smaller version: private life protected, public work visible, sources open, corrections welcome.

Stage 2

Aotearoa New Zealand comparison

Aotearoa is a sovereign comparison field with its own law, culture, constitutional memory, tangata whenua authority and political operating system.

Stage 3

Pacific listening layer

Pacific communities bring ocean knowledge, climate reality, language, sovereignty, migration intelligence and data governance lessons the whole region needs.

Stage 4

Oceania commons

Shared problems become shared quests: food waste, disaster resilience, care work, civic compute, ocean systems, open science and public-good contribution.

Stage 5

Oceania surge shelf

Space, deep infrastructure, health technology, digital twins and Kardashev-scale ambition get a real room with real research gates and real public imagination.

Plain rule

Local authority is the amplifier.

The strongest regional system is not the one that centralises everyone. It is the one that lets each place hold its own centre, then connect through receipts, protocols and shared build lanes.
