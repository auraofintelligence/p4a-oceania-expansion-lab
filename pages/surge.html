Oceania surge, fully labelled.

This is the big room: a 2028-2032 civic mobilisation arc that braids care receipts, local-first compute, food rescue, disaster resilience, health infrastructure review, deep materials sovereignty and space-scale imagination.

2028-2032 arc

Use Brisbane 2032 as the visible horizon.

The source memo treats Brisbane 2032 as a forcing function. This page turns that into a public design horizon: not a government plan, but a mobilisation story big enough to organise research, prototypes and civic imagination.

Care

C-Hour recognition layer

Make unpaid and under-recognised care, repair, mentoring, volunteering, disaster readiness and ecological work visible through consent-aware receipts and public-good ledgers.

Food

Waste-to-resilience routing

Use supply-chain twins, local kitchens, community groups and public records to ask where food is being lost, who can move it, and what infrastructure would stop the bleed.

Compute

Civic mesh before mega-cloud

Build the L0 to L3 compute gradient: personal context, neighbourhood nodes, council-scale reservoirs and wider systems that can survive stress without one fragile tether.

Disaster

Permanent volunteer capacity

Instead of dissolving major-event volunteer energy after the closing ceremony, ask how trained people, civic twins and C-Hour records could become standing resilience capacity.

Health

Evidence-gated health infrastructure

The source memo goes hard on sauna, HBOT and biological optimisation. This site keeps that energy on a review-gated shelf: medical evidence, equity, consent and safety before any public programme.

Deep

Subterranean resilience and materials

Heavy mineral sands, silica, thermal storage, hardened civic infrastructure and deep-time risk become design prompts for serious research into resilience, not instant mandates.

Orbit

Space-support civic workforce

The space shelf asks whether Oceania could train a human-in-the-loop support layer for orbital robotics, solar weather awareness, public science and future commons infrastructure.

Culture

Conversion through joy

Music, games, satire, public challenges and visible volunteering translate abstract governance into lived participation, while compliance records keep the funnel clean.

Political architecture

Coalition glue, not empire cosplay.

The Purple strategy in the source memo is not majority-rule fantasy. It is leverage architecture: be useful enough, transparent enough and compliance-native enough that larger blocs have to answer the public-good receipts.

The Oceania version sharpens that into a question: what if a political project measured care, resilience and truthfulness so clearly that confidence-and-supply became a civic infrastructure bargain?

Operating principle

Do not shrink the idea. Mark the shelf.

The lab can hold radical ambition if each shelf is marked honestly. C-Hours are a concept until governed. Health claims need evidence. Space ideas need technical review. Electoral claims need law. Cultural translation needs local authority. The labels are the container that lets the moonshot breathe.
