# Oceania Surge Notes

The surge shelf should not be timid.

It should hold:

- C-Hour recognition for care, repair, volunteering, ecological work and disaster readiness
- local-first civic compute
- food waste and supply-chain routing
- Brisbane 2032 as a public horizon
- standing volunteer capacity
- evidence-gated health infrastructure
- deep resilience and materials research
- space-support workforce imagination
- ethical overcompliance and public ledgers
- culture, music, games and public challenges as translation pathways

The rule is not "make it small". The rule is "mark the shelf".

Use bold language, but do not claim registration, authority, medical proof, legal status, funding, procurement or public consent.
